# DreamStation — Onboarding Intake Specification
**Full Programme | 10-Family Trial | Version 1.0 | March 2026**

---

## Purpose & Design Principles

This document specifies the onboarding intake flow for DreamStation Full Programme mode. It defines every screen, every question, all copy, field types, data destinations, and developer notes required to build the intake without additional product decisions being made during development.

### What This Intake Must Do

Capture enough context about the child and family to generate morning observations that feel specific and personally accurate — not generic. The bar is: would a parent think *"how did it know that?"* If the intake is too shallow the observations will be obvious and parents will stop engaging. If the intake is too long parents will drop off before completing it.

Based on the 30-night validation study, five areas of information are load-bearing for observation quality. Everything else is either generated by the child through usage or is noise. This intake captures only what is necessary.

### Design Principles

- **Conversational not clinical.** Every question should feel like a curious person asking about your child, not a form asking for data. Copy is warm, specific, and occasionally uses humour where appropriate.
- **Completable in under 10 minutes on a phone.** If it feels longer than that, parents will not finish it. Each section should feel like 90 seconds.
- **No wrong answers framing throughout.** Parents of neurodivergent children are often carrying guilt and hypervigilance. The intake should feel like a relief to complete, not an assessment.
- **Everything editable later.** No intake field should feel permanent. Settings should allow editing of all dynamic fields — current stressors, social world, friends — because these change.
- **Do not capture what the child will generate better.** Villain name, superpower, story preferences, ritual archetype — these emerge through usage and the child's own creation is always more accurate than a parent's prediction at onboarding.

## Intake Flow Overview

The intake runs immediately after mode selection — when a parent chooses Full Programme over Just Stories. Five sections presented as separate screens with a progress indicator throughout.

| Screen | Section | Est. Time | Required |
|--------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Who is your child | 2 min | Yes |
| 2 | Their nervous system | 2 min | Yes |
| 3 | What bedtime looks like | 1 min | Yes |
| 4 | What they worry about | 2 min | Yes |
| 5 | Their world | 2 min | Yes |

A sixth screen — the baseline capture — runs at the end of onboarding and again at Week 3. Specified at the end of this document.

## Section 1 — Who Is Your Child

Captures core identity. Feeds into every story prompt as the foundational character layer. The free-text description is the most valuable field in the entire intake — more useful than any structured field because parents reach for what is most true when speaking naturally.

### SCREEN: 1 of 5 — Who is your child?

**UI Copy:**
> Let's start with the basics. We'll use these to build a world that feels like it was made just for them.

---

**Field: What's their name?**
- Hint: *First name only — this appears in every story*
- Type: Text input, single line, required
- Saves to: `child_profile.name` 

---

**Field: How old are they?**
- Type: Number input or age picker, required
- Options: 3–12. Outside range: show message *"DreamStation works best for ages 3–12"*
- Saves to: `child_profile.age` 

---

**Field: Are they a girl, boy, or something else?**
- Hint: *This helps us get pronouns right in the stories*
- Type: Single select
- Options: `Girl` / `Boy` / `We use they/them` / `Skip this` 
- Saves to: `child_profile.gender_pronoun` 

---

**Field: Describe them in a sentence — the way you'd describe them to someone who'd never met them.**
- Hint: *e.g. "She's incredibly imaginative but finds the world a bit overwhelming sometimes" or "He's the funniest kid in the room until he's not, and then everything falls apart"*
- Type: Free text, multiline, required. Min 10 chars.
- Saves to: `child_profile.parent_description` 

---

**Dev notes:**
- Progress indicator: 1 of 5
- CTA: `Next` 
- `parent_description` is injected verbatim into the LLM system prompt as the primary character framing. Do not truncate.
- Validate age is between 3 and 12 before allowing progression

## Section 2 — Their Nervous System

Captures neuro and sensory context. The single most important section for observation quality. Knowing a child has sensory sensitivities changes the interpretation of nearly every behaviour the app will observe. Designed to be inclusive of undiagnosed and query families — "not sure" is a first-class option throughout.

### SCREEN: 2 of 5 — How does their nervous system work?

**UI Copy:**
> Every child's nervous system is different. This helps us understand how yours experiences the world — so the stories and the observations we share with you actually make sense for them.
>
> Tick anything that feels familiar. You don't need a diagnosis.

---

**Field: Do any of these sound like your child?**
- Hint: *Select all that apply*
- Type: Multi-select checklist
- Options:
  - Finds it hard to sit still or slow down
  - Gets overwhelmed by noise, crowds, or busy environments
  - Worries more than other kids their age
  - Finds transitions and changes to routine really difficult
  - Gets very focused on specific interests
  - Finds social situations draining even if they enjoy them
  - Finds it hard to get to sleep most nights
  - Has been assessed for or diagnosed with ADHD
  - Has been assessed for or diagnosed with autism
  - Has been assessed for or diagnosed with anxiety
  - Has sensory sensitivities (textures, sounds, tags, lights)
  - None of these really fit
  - Not sure
- Saves to: `child_profile.neuro_flags[]` (array of selected strings)

---

**Field: Anything else you'd like us to know about how they experience the world?**
- Hint: *Optional — anything not covered above*
- Type: Free text, multiline, optional
- Saves to: `child_profile.neuro_notes` 

---

**Dev notes:**
- Progress indicator: 2 of 5
- CTA: `Next` 
- Both fields injected into LLM system prompt as `neuro_context` block
- If `None of these really fit` is selected, disable all other options
- If `Not sure` is selected alongside others, that is valid — do not force a single selection
- `neuro_flags` array is used by the observation engine to contextualise behaviour — e.g. if "overwhelmed by noise" is flagged, a story featuring noise themes triggers a higher-weight observation

## Section 3 — What Bedtime Looks Like

Establishes the baseline and primes parents to notice change. Two questions only. The first captures the qualitative shape of the problem — the specific behaviours — which is more useful for observation generation than a rating scale. The second captures frequency of night waking which is the primary quantitative metric.

### SCREEN: 3 of 5 — Tell us about bedtime

**UI Copy:**
> Honestly — what does a hard bedtime look like in your house? Don't worry, we've heard it all.

---

**Field: When bedtime is difficult, what does that usually look like?**
- Hint: *e.g. "She finds a reason to get up every five minutes" or "He completely loses it if the routine changes even slightly" or "She just cannot switch her brain off"*
- Type: Free text, multiline, required
- Saves to: `baseline.bedtime_description` 

---

**Field: How often does your child wake in the night?**
- Type: Single select
- Options: `Almost every night` / `A few times a week` / `Once or twice a week` / `Occasionally` / `Rarely or never` 
- Saves to: `baseline.night_waking_frequency` 

---

**Dev notes:**
- Progress indicator: 3 of 5
- CTA: `Next` 
- `bedtime_description` is stored as baseline and surfaced in the weekly brief when patterns change
- `night_waking_frequency` maps to numeric: Almost every night = 5, A few times a week = 3–4, Once or twice = 1–2, Occasionally = <1, Rarely = 0
- These fields also populate the clinical baseline score — see Section 6
- Both fields are read-only after onboarding — update only via Settings > Edit Profile > Bedtime baseline

---

## Section 4 — What They Worry About

The fear and stressor landscape. The most clinically significant section for the observation engine. Knowing a child's active fears allows the LLM to flag when those themes appear in story prompts — which is where the most diagnostically rich observations come from. The 30-night study showed that children surface fears through story choice long before they name them directly.

Two parts: the standing fear landscape (relatively stable) and the live stressor field (dynamic, editable, changes weekly).

### SCREEN: 4 of 5 — What's on their mind?

**UI Copy:**
> Children often show us what they're worried about before they tell us. This helps us notice when those themes come up in their stories.

---

**Field: Does your child worry about any of these? Tick anything that feels true.**
- Hint: *You know them best — go with your gut*
- Type: Multi-select checklist
- Options:
  - Starting something new (school, activity, place)
  - Making or keeping friends
  - Getting things wrong or not being good enough
  - Loud or overwhelming environments
  - Something bad happening to someone they love
  - Their body (illness, pain, physical changes)
  - Being left out or excluded
  - The dark or being alone at night
  - Changes to routine or plans
  - Being watched or performing in front of others
  - Not sure
  - None of these
- Saves to: `child_profile.fear_flags[]` (array)

---

**Field: Is there anything happening in their life right now that we should know about?**
- Hint: *e.g. "She's nervous about a big family event next week" or "His best friend just moved schools" or "We have a new baby at home". Big things and small things both count.*
- Type: Free text, multiline, optional
- Saves to: `child_profile.current_stressors` (string, editable)

---

**Dev notes:**
- Progress indicator: 4 of 5
- CTA: `Next` 
- `fear_flags` array is highest-weight input for observation generation — when a story prompt contains themes matching a fear flag, the observation engine prioritises surfacing it
- `current_stressors` is dynamic — prompt parent to update this field in the weekly brief if it hasn't been edited in 14 days
- `current_stressors` appears prominently in Settings as "What's happening this week" — should feel like a living field not a one-time capture
- If `None of these` selected, disable other options

---

## Section 5 — Their World

Captures the social and relational context. Allies become recurring story characters. The social difficulty field feeds the observation engine for pattern-spotting across sessions. Should feel the lightest of the five — by this point parents have answered the hard questions.

### SCREEN: 5 of 5 — Their world

**UI Copy:**
> Almost done. This helps us put the right people in their stories — and notice when they keep coming up.

---

**Field: Who are the important people in their life?**
- Hint: *Add family members, close friends, anyone who matters to them*
- Type: Dynamic list — add person by name and relationship. Min 1, no max.
- Options (relationship): `Parent` / `Sibling` / `Grandparent` / `Friend` / `Teacher` / `Other` 
- Saves to: `child_profile.allies[]` `{name, relationship}` 

---

**Field: Any pets?**
- Type: Dynamic list — name and type of animal. Optional.
- Saves to: `child_profile.pets[]` `{name, type}` 

---

**Field: Is there anyone or anything causing difficulty for them socially right now?**
- Hint: *e.g. "There's a kid at school who's been unkind" or "She's finding it hard since her best friend moved class". Optional — skip if nothing comes to mind.*
- Type: Free text, multiline, optional
- Saves to: `child_profile.social_difficulty` (string, editable)

---

**Dev notes:**
- Progress indicator: 5 of 5
- CTA: `Finish — let's make some stories` 
- `allies` array feeds directly into Gemini story prompt as named supporting characters
- Allies added here appear in story generation as options the child can include
- `social_difficulty` is dynamic — same update prompt logic as `current_stressors` 
- After tapping Finish, navigate to the completion screen before going to home

## Section 6 — Clinical Baseline Capture

Separate from the intake flow but triggered immediately after the completion screen. Repeated at Week 3. This is the outcome measurement layer — it gives the weekly brief trend data and gives the trial quantitative before/after comparisons.

Presented as a separate short screen after the completion screen. Should feel light and quick. Sliders are appropriate here because the parent has already done the qualitative work in the intake.

### SCREEN: One last thing — a quick snapshot

**UI Copy:**
> We'll ask you these same questions in three weeks so we can show you what's changed. Takes about a minute.

---

**Field: The bedtime battle — how hard is it right now?**
- Hint: *1 = barely a struggle, 10 = full war every night*
- Type: Slider 1–10
- Saves to: `baseline_scores.bedtime_resistance_score` + timestamp

---

**Field: Time to fall asleep — once they're settled, how long does it usually take?**
- Hint: *1 = out quickly, 10 = takes forever*
- Type: Slider 1–10
- Saves to: `baseline_scores.sleep_onset_score` + timestamp

---

**Field: Night wakings — how often are you up in the night?**
- Hint: *1 = rarely, 10 = most nights*
- Type: Slider 1–10
- Saves to: `baseline_scores.night_waking_score` + timestamp

---

**Field: Your stress at bedtime — how are you finding it?**
- Hint: *1 = fine, 10 = I'm on my last nerve by 8pm*
- Type: Slider 1–10
- Saves to: `baseline_scores.parent_stress_score` + timestamp

---

**Field: Their mood in the morning — how do they usually start the day?**
- Hint: *1 = bright and easy, 10 = really hard to get going*
- Type: Slider 1–10
- Saves to: `baseline_scores.morning_mood_score` + timestamp

---

**Dev notes:**
- CTA: `Done — let's go` 
- Store all five scores with `measurement_type: 'baseline'` and timestamp
- Re-trigger this exact screen at Day 21 with `measurement_type: 'week3'` 
- Week 3 re-capture should be prompted via push notification and in-app banner — not buried in Settings
- Delta between baseline and week3 scores feeds the weekly brief trend language
- Do not show scores back to the parent as a dashboard — surface only as language in the weekly brief e.g. *"Bedtime has been getting easier"* not *"Your score improved from 8 to 5"*

## Gemini System Prompt — Intake Fields Injection

Every Full Programme story generation call injects the child profile as structured context:

```
CHILD PROFILE:
Name: {name}, Age: {age}
About them: {parent_description}
Nervous system: {neuro_flags joined}, {neuro_notes}
Known fears and sensitivities: {fear_flags joined}
What's happening in their life right now: {current_stressors}
Social difficulty: {social_difficulty}

ALLIES (supporting characters — they help but never solve):
{allies map: Name — relationship}
{pets map: Name — type}

TONIGHT:
Child's story prompt: {story_prompt}
Parent's note: {parent_note}
Mode: {co-creation | playback}

STORY RULES:
- The problem is never the child — externalise it as a character or force
- The child resolves the story using their own strengths
- Allies support but never rescue
- End with the child safe, settled, the problem small and far away
- Final 2 minutes: slow, hypnotic, repetitive language — heavy limbs, slow breath
- Final line: "And then, {name} was fast asleep."
```

## Open Questions for Trial Build

**1. Onboarding for multiple children**
The spec assumes one child per account. If a family has two children in the trial age range, does each child get a separate profile under one parent account? This affects the allies model (siblings appear as allies in each other's profiles) and the notification logic.

**2. Skipping Section 4 entirely**
If a parent selects "None of these" for `fear_flags` and leaves `current_stressors` blank, the observation engine has very limited material. Does this trigger a prompt to revisit, or does it fall back to story-content-only observations?

**3. current_stressors update prompt cadence**
The spec says prompt to update if not edited in 14 days. Should this be a push notification, an in-app banner on next open, or surfaced in the weekly brief? Recommendation: weekly brief, lower friction.

**4. Allies in story generation**
Does the child see the allies list and choose to include them, or does Gemini incorporate them automatically? The 30-night study suggests child choice produces more authentic engagement — recommend surfacing allies as options during co-creation rather than auto-injecting.

**5. Baseline re-capture at Week 3**
Automatic push notification or parent-initiated? Recommendation: push notification with in-app fallback. If parent does not complete within 3 days of prompt, flag in weekly brief data but do not block app usage.
